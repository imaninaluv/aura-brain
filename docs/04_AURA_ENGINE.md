# AURA Engine

Version: 1.0  
Status: Locked  
Last Updated: 2026-07-05

---

# Purpose

AURA (Analyze, Understand, Review, Approve) is Aura AI's internal content validation engine.

Unlike HERO, AURA does not generate content from scratch.

Its responsibility is to review, refine, and approve HERO's output before it reaches the user.

AURA acts as both a reader and an editor.

It reads content from the reader's perspective while improving content from an experienced editor's perspective.

AURA is never exposed to users.

It operates entirely behind the scenes.

---

# Definition

AURA is an AI quality control engine designed to ensure every generated thread follows Aura Brain standards before being presented to the user.

AURA focuses on:

- Writing quality
- Reader psychology
- Writing Mode compliance
- HERO methodology
- Human writing
- Content flow
- Overall readability

AURA does not create new content ideas.

It improves the ideas already generated by HERO.

---

# Core Philosophy

AURA exists to improve—not replace.

Its purpose is to polish HERO's output rather than regenerate everything from the beginning.

Only weak sections are improved.

Everything else remains untouched.

The creator always makes the final decision.

---

# Responsibilities

AURA is responsible for:

- Reviewing HERO's output
- Detecting weak sections
- Improving individual sections
- Validating Writing Mode objectives
- Validating HERO methodology
- Performing quality control
- Assigning Aura Score
- Returning only approved content

AURA is NOT responsible for:

- Generating new thread ideas
- Rewriting the entire thread
- Editing user-modified drafts
- Scheduling
- Publishing
- Predicting virality

---

# AURA Workflow

Every validation follows four internal stages.

```
Analyze

↓

Understand

↓

Review

↓

Approve
```

---

# Stage 1 — Analyze

Analyze performs the initial scan of HERO's output.

Checks include:

- Writing Mode
- Topic consistency
- HERO structure
- Thread progression
- Human readability
- Overall clarity

Analyze never modifies content.

Its purpose is observation only.

---

# Stage 2 — Understand

Understand identifies sections that require improvement.

Examples include:

- Weak Hook
- Weak Engage
- Weak Reveal
- Weak Outcome
- Weak curiosity
- Weak emotional progression
- Generic AI wording
- Wrong Writing Mode behaviour
- Wrong bait
- Weak thread flow

Only sections that require improvement are regenerated.

The entire thread is never regenerated.

If additional explanation improves the thread, AURA may expand:

```
Reveal

↓

Reveal 1

↓

Reveal 2
```

This decision is made automatically.

---

# Stage 3 — Review

After improvements are completed, AURA performs one final review.

Review validates:

- Writing Mode objective
- HERO methodology
- Reader progression
- Human writing quality
- Overall thread quality

Review happens only once.

If a rewritten section still has minor imperfections but achieves acceptable quality, the thread proceeds to approval.

AURA does not repeatedly regenerate content.

The objective is efficient optimisation rather than perfection.

---

# Stage 4 — Approve

Once Review is complete, AURA determines whether the thread is suitable for delivery.

If the thread satisfies Aura Brain standards:

Approved

↓

Return to user

If the thread does not satisfy the minimum standard:

Improve affected section

↓

Review

↓

Approve

This optimisation happens only once during the generation pipeline.

Users never see rejected or intermediate versions.

---

# Validation Priority

When multiple issues exist, AURA always prioritises them in the following order:

1. Writing Mode compliance

2. HERO methodology

3. Reader psychology

4. Human writing quality

5. Grammar and wording

Psychology is always prioritised over grammar.

Perfect grammar does not guarantee engaging content.

---

# Writing Mode Validation

AURA validates every thread according to the selected Writing Mode.

---

## Selling Mode

Goal

Sell the solution.

Never sell the product.

Validation checks include:

- Is the solution clearly communicated?
- Does the reader imagine a better outcome?
- Does the CTA feel natural?
- Is curiosity created before action?

---

## Informative Mode

Goal

Help readers gain a new perspective.

Validation checks include:

- Does the reader learn something valuable?
- Is the explanation clear?
- Does the thread encourage discussion?
- Does the reader finish feeling smarter?

---

## Engagement Mode

Goal

Create opinions.

Validation checks include:

- Does the thread encourage comments?
- Is the final question engaging?
- Does the topic invite multiple perspectives?
- Will readers naturally want to participate?

---

# Reader Simulation

AURA reviews content as if it were the target audience.

Instead of checking only grammar, AURA asks:

- Would I continue reading?
- Do I understand the message?
- Does this feel relatable?
- Does this create curiosity?
- Would I leave a comment?
- Does this sound human?

Reader experience is prioritised over grammatical perfection.

---

# Editor Simulation

After reading the thread, AURA switches to editor mode.

The editor focuses on:

- Improving weak wording
- Improving flow
- Strengthening curiosity
- Strengthening psychological progression
- Improving clarity
- Removing repetitive wording

The editor never changes the original objective of the thread.

---

# Aura Score

After validation, every thread receives one overall Aura Score.

The score represents overall compliance with Aura Brain standards.

Aura Score is holistic.

It does not calculate individual scores for Hook, Engage, Reveal, or Outcome separately.

The purpose of Aura Score is simple:

Help users identify which generated thread should be prioritised for publishing.

Aura Score does not predict performance or virality.

---

# Internal Validation Log

During validation, AURA generates internal reasoning.

Example:

Weak Hook

Reason:

Curiosity not established.

These internal logs exist only for development, debugging, and future improvements.

They are never shown to users.

---

# User Editing

Once approved content reaches the user:

AURA's responsibility ends.

If the user edits the thread:

```
Approved Thread

↓

User Edit

↓

Draft Mode
```

The edited version belongs entirely to the user.

AURA never validates user-modified drafts.

---

# Quality Control Philosophy

AURA behaves like an experienced senior editor.

Its responsibility is not to make content perfect.

Its responsibility is to ensure the content is strong enough before it reaches the creator.

Perfection is not the objective.

Consistency, clarity, and reader retention are.

---

# Engine Principle

HERO creates.

AURA critiques.

HERO writes.

AURA refines.

HERO generates.

AURA approves.

Together, they produce content that is psychologically structured, human, and ready for the creator to review.

The creator always has the final decision.
