# Scoring System

Version: 1.0  
Status: Locked  
Last Updated: 2026-07-05

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Aura AI evaluates content quality before presenting it to users.

The scoring system exists to help creators identify which generated thread should be prioritised for publishing.

Aura Score is not designed to predict virality.

Its purpose is to measure how well the generated content follows Aura Brain standards.

---

# Philosophy

Aura Score is a quality indicator.

It is not a performance predictor.

A high Aura Score means the content closely follows:

- Writing Mode objectives
- HERO methodology
- Reader psychology
- Human writing standards
- Aura Brain principles

A thread with a perfect Aura Score is not guaranteed to go viral.

Likewise, a lower score does not mean the content will perform poorly.

Aura Score measures writing quality, not platform performance.

---

# Scoring Philosophy

Aura AI does not score content section-by-section.

Instead, AURA evaluates the thread holistically.

The AI reviews the complete reading experience before assigning a single overall score.

This allows the score to better reflect how real readers experience the content.

---

# Internal Evaluation Categories

Although users only see one overall score, AURA internally evaluates multiple quality dimensions.

These include:

- Writing Mode Compliance
- HERO Methodology Compliance
- Reader Retention
- Human Writing Quality
- Psychological Flow
- Overall Content Quality

These categories are used internally during validation.

Individual category scores are never exposed to users.

---

# Aura Score Scale

Aura Score ranges from 0 to 100.

However, users will only receive approved threads.

Threads scoring below 80 are automatically improved by AURA before being returned.

As a result, users will never receive content below the minimum quality threshold.

---

## 95–100

Status:

Excellent

Characteristics:

- Exceptional reader progression
- Strong psychological flow
- Natural human writing
- Fully aligned with Writing Mode
- Highly recommended for publishing

---

## 90–94

Status:

Strong

Characteristics:

- High-quality content
- Minor opportunities for improvement
- Strong reader retention
- Clear messaging

Suitable for publishing without modification.

---

## 80–89

Status:

Good

Characteristics:

- Meets Aura Brain standards
- Writing Mode achieved
- Readable and coherent
- May contain small imperfections that do not significantly affect quality

Suitable for publishing.

---

## Below 80

Status:

Internal Only

Threads below 80 are never shown to users.

Instead:

AURA identifies weak sections

↓

Improves only those sections

↓

Performs one final review

↓

Returns the improved version

Users never interact with low-quality output.

---

# Validation Process

Aura Score is assigned only after AURA completes its validation workflow.

The process follows:

Analyze

↓

Understand

↓

Review

↓

Assign Aura Score

↓

Approve

The score always represents the final approved version.

---

# Reader-Centric Evaluation

Aura Score is generated from the perspective of a reader.

Rather than focusing only on grammar or structure, AURA asks questions such as:

- Would I continue reading?
- Does this feel natural?
- Does this create curiosity?
- Does this achieve the intended Writing Mode?
- Would this encourage the desired reader action?
- Does this sound like a real creator?

The objective is to simulate a genuine reading experience.

---

# Quality Threshold

Aura AI follows a minimum quality threshold.

If the generated content scores below 80:

Only weak sections are improved.

The entire thread is never regenerated.

After improvements are completed:

The thread proceeds directly to approval.

AURA performs only one optimisation cycle.

The objective is fast generation while maintaining high quality.

---

# User Experience

Users never see the validation process.

They only receive:

- Approved content
- Overall Aura Score
- Score status

Example:

96

Excellent

or

91

Strong

or

84

Good

The scoring system exists to simplify decision making.

Creators should immediately know which generated thread deserves priority.

---

# Recommended Thread

When multiple threads are generated, Aura AI automatically highlights the highest-scoring thread.

This thread receives the Recommended badge.

Example:

⭐ Recommended

Aura Score: 96

The Recommended badge is intended only as guidance.

Creators remain free to publish any generated thread regardless of its score.

---

# Internal Validation Logs

During evaluation, AURA generates internal validation logs.

Examples include:

Weak Hook

Reason:

Curiosity not established.

---

Weak Outcome

Reason:

Selling Mode objective not fully achieved.

These logs exist solely for:

- Internal debugging
- Future improvements
- AI behaviour analysis
- Developer reference

Validation logs are never displayed to users.

---

# What Aura Score Does NOT Measure

Aura Score does not evaluate:

- Virality
- Platform algorithm behaviour
- Future impressions
- Reach
- Likes
- Shares
- Comments
- Business performance

Those outcomes depend on many external factors beyond writing quality.

Aura Score evaluates only compliance with Aura Brain standards.

---

# Engine Principle

Aura Score exists to reduce creator uncertainty.

Instead of asking:

"Which thread should I publish?"

Creators should immediately recognise the strongest option.

Aura AI simplifies the decision.

The creator makes the final choice.
