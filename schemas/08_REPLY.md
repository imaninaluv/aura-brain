Status: Locked

# Purpose

The Reply Schema defines creator engagement within Aura AI.

Its purpose is to represent replies generated through the Scout feature and published in response to existing Threads conversations.

Unlike the Thread Schema, which focuses on creating original content, the Reply Schema manages conversational engagement by allowing creators to review, edit, and publish AI-generated replies.

---

# Definition

A Reply represents a creator's response to an existing Threads post.

Replies are generated by Aura AI through the Scout feature and may be edited before publication.

A Reply allows creators to:

- Review AI-generated replies.
- Edit reply content inline.
- Publish replies immediately.

Replies are designed for real-time engagement.

They do not support Drafts, scheduling, or long-term editing workflows.

Each Reply maintains the same identity throughout its lifecycle until publication.

---

# Scope

The Reply Schema collaborates with multiple business schemas throughout Aura AI.

These include:

- Scout
- Planner
- Stats

Reply is responsible for:

- Managing AI-generated reply content.
- Supporting inline editing before publication.
- Publishing replies to Threads.
- Recording successful published replies.

Reply does not generate Scout opportunities.

Reply does not create original Threads.

Reply does not support Drafts or scheduling.

Its responsibility is limited to managing the creator's engagement workflow from generated reply to published conversation.

Aura AI separates original content creation from conversational engagement.

```
Scout
   │
   ▼
Generate Reply
   │
   ▼
Inline Edit (Optional)
   │
   ▼
Publish
   │
   ▼
Published Reply
```

Replies are intentionally lightweight.

Creators may make quick edits before publishing, but every Reply is designed to support immediate participation in ongoing conversations.

By removing unnecessary steps such as Drafts and scheduling, Aura AI provides a fast and frictionless engagement experience while maintaining a consistent business architecture.

# Schema Overview

The Reply Schema represents a creator's engagement with an existing Threads conversation.

Every Reply originates from a Scout opportunity and contains AI-generated content that may be edited before publication.

Unlike Threads, Replies are designed for immediate interaction and therefore support a much simpler lifecycle.

Each Reply maintains a single identity from generation until publication.

# Core Properties

Each Reply maintains the information required to support the creator's engagement workflow.

Core properties include:

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Reply ID | Unique identifier for the Reply. |
| Account Context ID | The Account Context that owns the Reply. |
| Scout ID | Identifier of the Scout opportunity that generated the Reply. |
| Target Post ID | Identifier of the Threads post being replied to. |
| Reply Content | Current editable reply content. |
| Published At | Date and time the Reply was successfully published. |
| Created At | Date and time the Reply was first generated. |
| Last Edited At | Date and time the Reply was last modified before publication. |

These properties allow Aura AI to manage creator engagement while maintaining a clear relationship between Scout, Reply, and the original Threads conversation.

Instead, it provides a lightweight engagement workflow from AI-generated suggestion to published conversation.

# Lifecycle

The Reply Schema manages the creator's engagement lifecycle from AI-generated suggestion to published conversation.

Unlike Threads, Replies are intended for immediate interaction and therefore follow a simplified lifecycle.

The typical lifecycle is:

```
Scout Opportunity
        │
        ▼
Generate Reply
        │
        ▼

        │
        ▼
```

A Reply may be edited multiple times before publication.

Each edit updates the existing Reply without creating a new Reply object.

The Reply lifecycle ends once the Reply has been successfully published or discarded.

# Editing Behaviour

Replies support lightweight inline editing before publication.

Creators may:

- Edit any portion of the generated reply.
- Continue editing without entering a dedicated editing mode.
- Publish the edited reply immediately.

Replies intentionally do not support:

- Saving Drafts.
- Scheduling publication.
- Reopening discarded Replies.

If the creator chooses not to publish the Reply and moves to another Scout opportunity, the current Reply is discarded.

This behaviour keeps the engagement workflow fast, lightweight, and aligned with real-time conversations.

# Business Rules

The Reply Schema follows a set of rules that ensure creator engagement remains fast, consistent, and aligned with real-time conversations.

## Rule 1 — Every Reply Originates from Scout

Every Reply must originate from exactly one Scout opportunity.

Replies cannot be created independently.

Aura AI always prepares the initial Reply before the creator begins editing or publishing.

---

## Rule 2 — Replies Support Inline Editing

Creators may edit any part of a generated Reply before publication.

Editing occurs directly within the Reply without requiring a dedicated editing mode.

Every edit updates the current Reply.

Aura AI must never create duplicate Replies while editing.

---

## Rule 3 — Replies Do Not Support Drafts or Scheduling

Replies are designed for immediate engagement.

Replies must never support:

- Drafts.
- Scheduled publication.
- Reopening after being discarded.

This keeps the engagement workflow simple and responsive.

---

## Rule 4 — Published Replies Become Permanent Records

Once successfully published, a Reply becomes a permanent business object.

Published Replies are recorded within Planner and contribute to account performance within Stats.

Only successfully published Replies become part of the creator's permanent activity history.

---

## Rule 5 — Discarded Replies Are Not Preserved

If the creator decides not to publish the current Reply, Aura AI discards it when:

- A new Reply is generated.
- The creator moves to another Scout opportunity.
- The current engagement session ends.

Discarded Replies do not create business history and cannot be recovered.

---

# Validation Rules

Before publishing a Reply, Aura AI should validate that:

- A valid Account Context is active.
- The originating Scout opportunity is valid.
- The target Threads post is still available.
- The Reply contains valid content.
- The connected Threads account is available.

If validation fails, Aura AI should prevent publication while preserving the current editable Reply so the creator may continue editing or retry publishing.

These validation rules ensure Replies are published only when both the conversation and the creator's account remain valid.

# Business Examples

The following examples demonstrate how the Reply Schema behaves during normal creator workflows.

### Example 1 — Publishing a Generated Reply

A creator opens the Scout feature.

Aura AI presents a Threads post together with a generated Reply.

The creator reviews the suggested Reply and publishes it immediately without making any changes.

Aura AI publishes the Reply successfully.

The published Reply is then recorded within Planner and contributes to future account statistics.

---

### Example 2 — Editing Before Publishing

A creator reviews the generated Reply but decides to make several wording adjustments.

The creator edits the Reply directly within the text area.

No separate editing mode is required.

After completing the edits, the creator publishes the Reply.

Aura AI publishes the updated version while preserving the same Reply identity throughout the workflow.

---

### Example 3 — Discarding a Reply

A creator reviews the generated Reply but decides not to participate in the conversation.

The creator swipes to another Scout opportunity.

Aura AI discards the current Reply and immediately presents a newly generated Reply for the next opportunity.

The discarded Reply is not recorded within Planner and does not contribute to account statistics.

# Design Rationale

The Reply Schema is designed around real-time engagement rather than long-form content creation.

Unlike Threads, which may require multiple editing sessions, Drafts, and scheduled publication, Replies are intentionally lightweight.

Creators receive an AI-generated suggestion, make quick inline edits if necessary, and immediately participate in the conversation.

By removing Drafts, scheduling, and complex editing workflows, Aura AI reduces friction while preserving enough flexibility for creators to personalise every Reply before publication.

This approach provides:

- Fast conversational engagement.
- Minimal publishing friction.
- Consistent inline editing.
- Clear separation from Thread publishing workflows.
- Efficient integration with Scout, Planner, and Stats.

The Reply Schema represents Aura AI's conversational engagement layer.
